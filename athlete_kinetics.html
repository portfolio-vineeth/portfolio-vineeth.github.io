Athlete Kinetics

In our project, we employed pose estimation networks to extract anthropometric data, which was then analyzed using machine learning to predict the height of a jump. Our focus was on key biomechanical factors such as knee flexion, ankle flexion, and stomach bend. By capturing these specific body movements and postures through advanced pose estimation technology, we gathered comprehensive data on the mechanics of jumping. This data was fed into a machine learning model to establish correlations between the observed body flexions and the resultant jump height.
