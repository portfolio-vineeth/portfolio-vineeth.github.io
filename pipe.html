Principal Stresses in Pipe using Computer Vision

In our project, we innovated a method to evaluate stresses and deformations in pipelines using computer vision and machine learning. Targeting the maintenance of crucial fluid transport pipelines, our approach utilized custom object detection models to identify and analyze pipes in images.

Our process involved five stages: image capture of the pipe, extraction of properties like radius and material, stress calculation (hoop, longitudinal, and radial) based on internal and external pressures, and deformation analysis, especially for thin pipes. We created a specialized dataset for pipe detection, applied transfer learning for object detection, and used computer vision for accurate dimension measurement.
